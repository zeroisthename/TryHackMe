SOC is the central hub for an organization's cyber security - they are the first line of defense, work with various alerts, and handle most attacks
- **L1 Analysts**: Junior members who triage alerts and pass complex cases to L2
- **L2 Analysts:** Experienced members who investigate more advanced attacks
- **Engineers**: Experts in configuring security tools like or
- **Manager**: A person who manages the whole team

## Cyber Incident Response Team (CIRT)

![[Pasted image 20260613005300.png]]

If expertise is not enough or the incident goes out of control, you urgently call the "firefighters" - CIRT, also called or . The members should have a broad knowledge of cyber threats and handle breaches without depending on tools like or . A CIRT job is stressful and responsible, but also rewarding. Here are a few CIRT examples:

- [JPCERT (opens in new tab)](https://www.jpcert.or.jp/english): Japan's handling nation-wide breaches
- [Mandiant (opens in new tab)](https://www.mandiant.com): A private team responding to global cyber incidents
- [CIRT (opens in new tab)](https://aws.amazon.com/security-incident-response): Investigates security incidents of customers



## Specialized Defensive Roles
![[Pasted image 20260613005227.png]]
- **Digital Forensics Analyst**: Uncover hidden threats in disk and memory
- **Threat Intelligence Analyst**: Gather data about emerging threat groups
- **AppSec Engineer**: Maintain a secure software development lifecycle
- **Researcher**: Study threats and how to defend against them
